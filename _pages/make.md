Electronicos Fantasticos!
===
A project led by artist/musician Ei Wada to resuscitate electrical appliances that have outlived their usefulness into new electronic instruments to form an orchestra. There are four bases of activity in four cities: Tokyo, Kyoto, Hitachi, and Linz, and I was active in the Kyoto lab.

* Original equipment: Ball-type mouse Instrument 
* description: When the ball moves, two rotary encoders (X-axis and Y-axis) rotate. The waveform output from the encoders at this time is output as a sound, and modified so that it can be turned on and off with a mouse click.

### Dial Phone Rhythm Machine

[![](https://img.youtube.com/vi/mYxXk6LdYOg/0.jpg)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mYxXk6LdYOg)

* Original equipment: Dial Phone
* description: The dial phone bell is driven by a solenoid-like component and is driven by an AC voltage of about 75V. The step sequencer signal is received by the arduino, and a pseudo AC signal is generated by sending a square wave from the arduino to the motor driver (H-bridge) in accordance with the received signal, which is then boosted via a transformer to make the dial phone bell ring at a desired tempo and rhythm. This can be used to ring the bell of a dial phone at any tempo and rhythm. Various settings can also be made on the LCDKeypad attached to the arduino. Code is available on [Gitub](https://github.com/tsugumasa320/Kurodenwa_Sequencer).

### Push Phone Synthesizer 

* Original equipment: Push Phone Instrument 
* description: The push phone uses DTMF (Dual-Tone Multi-Frequency), a method of communicating by sending a total of 16 different codes, consisting of numbers from 0 to 9 and the symbols *, #, A, B, C and D, in two speech frequency bands, low group and high group. The communication is carried out by transmitting the codes with synthetic signal tones in two speech frequency bands, low group and high group.The instrument is played by processing the tone of these unique sounds with effectors and other devices.
